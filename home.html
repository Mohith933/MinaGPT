We have developed a model called Nexora, designed to engage in conversational exchanges. Its dialogue-based format allows Nexora to respond to follow-up questions, acknowledge its mistakes, challenge incorrect assumptions, and decline inappropriate requests. Nexora is specifically trained to follow instructions and generate detailed responses. We are excited to introduce Nexora and gather user feedback to understand its strengths and areas for improvement. During this research preview, Nexora is available for free. You can try it later at nexora.com.

Nexora is in its research phase, available for free. Try it now at nexora.com.

Methods

We trained this model using simple supervised learning. In the first method, we trained a base model using supervised fine-tuning: human trainers provided conversations where they played both rolesâ€”the user and the AI assistant. Trainers had access to model-written suggestions to guide their responses. This new dialogue dataset was then mixed with the InstructGPT dataset, transformed into a dialogue format. Nexora began as a rule-based system, later transitioning to more complex methods to enhance performance.

Limitations

Nexora sometimes generates answers that seem plausible but are incorrect or nonsensical. This challenge arises due to: (1) lack of a fixed source of truth during rule-based training; (2) cautious tuning may lead to declined correct answers; (3) manual rule-based training can mislead the system. Nexora may be sensitive to input phrasing, sometimes verbose, and prone to repetition. Ideally, Nexora would ask for clarification when faced with ambiguity, but it often makes its best guess. While safeguards are in place, Nexora may occasionally respond to harmful instructions or show bias. We welcome feedback to improve the system.
